communiT Boston

We strive to use art and play on the subway (the T) to foster empathy and create micro-connections between people who might otherwise never interact and may never meet again. Part community engagement project, part social experiment, and part free event planning organization, we’re here to break norms, connect people in new ways, and inject a little more fun and meaning into people’s commutes—whether that means giving people the opportunity to shred their sorrows in a paper shredder on the T platform or encouraging people to let loose on a glow-in-the-dark bubble wrap dance floor!

Icons

Celebrate Valentines Day with communiT Boston

We handed out free carnations with the caveat that recipients not keep the flowers for themselves, but instead challenge themselves to share the love by passing the carnations along to someone else, whether that was a stranger they ran into on their way home or whomever they were taking the T to meet. We also had a valentine-making station and a 60-second eye contact challenge.

Let Go & Look Up

What happens when you put a paper shredder on the T platform? We asked commuters to write down something that they wanted to let go of in their life and then gave them the carnal satisfaction of shredding it and claiming their own personal blank slate.

Tattoos with Strangers

We offered free temporary tattoos to commuters, with the caveat that they had to get a matching one with a stranger. People could pick from more than 200 options (hearts, dinosaurs, and Disney Princesses, anyone?) to commemorate their fledgling acquaintanceship with a passerby!

2 Strangers, 2 Tin Cans, and a String: A Social Experiment in Connection

We put away our smart phones and brought long-distance communication back to the basics. We asked strangers to have a casual chat with each other using the original walkie talkie: two cans and a piece of string!

A Pop-out, Pop-up Event

We invited people to let loose on our glow‑in‑the‑dark bubble wrap dance floor and experience the joy of popping along to the beat of the music.

The Worry Bead Project

We invited people to be part of our reworking of an ancient ritual by writing down their worries on the bead of your choice. The color of each bead served as a visual representation of the different categories of worries--relational, financial, spiritual, etc. Over the course of the afternoon, individuals’ internal experiences were transformed into a collective art piece.

TEA WITH STRANGERS

As a host for Tea With Strangers Boston, I bring together groups of 2 to 5 strangers for 2-hour long, free-flowing conversations in public settings. You never know who is going to show up to a tea time, and you never know what you’re all going to talk about!
